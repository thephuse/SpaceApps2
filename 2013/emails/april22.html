Space Apps Toronto: Mission Successful

2013 Has Launched!

We’ve had an incredible weekend hacking alongside 9000 international participants working on 600 projects, in 80+ cities. We’re honoured to have spent the weekend hacking with everyone, and are incredibly proud of what was created over the weekend. You’ve affected the way space data can be consumed and used in everyday life, made real research easier, and solidified Toronto’s place as one of the top Space Apps in the world.

We’d like to give one big final thank you to all our sponsors and volunteers, and give a special shout-out to Matt Potter of HTML5 Toronto who singlehandedly handled our livestream, greenscreen, and edited your pitch videos, thanks Matt!.

Judges Pick

We don’t have to tell you how high the standard was walking around Bronfman Hall. We’re thrilled at the final presentations, and can’t wait to see how they may evolve in the months ahead. Special kudos goes out to our judges’ picks below. Check out all the presentations here.

  • NASA International Candidate: Aurora Localization via Starfields
  • NASA International Candidate: Museum of Intergalactic Species
  • Best of in Visualization: Moon tour
  • Best of in Software: Waystation
  • Best of in Hardware: International Print Station
  • Best of in Hardware: Green mars
  • Best of in Citizen Science: SocialNEO

Keep us in the Loop

Tell us how you’re doing, and we promise to tell NASA and help where we can. Some teams have already received overtures to longer relationships with aerospace and product companies, and others are committed to continued development over the next few months. Keep us in the loop at info@spaceappstoronto.com.

Stay Ready for Next Year

We’re excited as you are for the 2014 Space Apps Challenge and are already working to take the Toronto event to the next level. We’ve updated our site to allow everyone to stay in touch for updates on next year’s event, and we encourage everyone to keep in touch.
