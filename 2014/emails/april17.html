T-Minus 2 Days!

What to Do Right Now

We've got 50 people on the waiting list. If you're not coming, please immediately delete your account with NASA by going into your Account Settings and clicking "Delete Account" at the top-right.

While you're waiting for the big kickoff day, check out the Google Group and get a head start discussing the challenges. You should also definitely check out the hardware tools, discounts, and services available for Space Apps participants (Leap Controllers & Bluetooth Sensors!!)

What To Bring

  • Computer
  • Backup internet connection if you have one, like a tethered phone or hotspot (just in case).
  • Notebook (analog)
  • Pencils/pens
  • Hardware developers who would like to request special tools should do by midnight on Wednesday, April 17.
  • A shirt, as we've had to cut the Space Apps t-shirt budget due to more pressing demands on our sponsorship bucks.

When and Where to Arrive

Registration opens Friday at 6pm, and the program kicks off at 7. Plan to arrive no later than 6:30 to ensure you can get through registration in time and don't miss critical info.

Please enter through the old East entrance facing University (Queens Park Avenue), the same entrance you remember from your Grade 6 field trip days. You will not be able to enter through the main crystal entrance. When you get in, you'll be directed to Bronfman Hall and to the registration table.

FAQs

Is food being provided?
Sadly not, as we've had to focus our limited sponsorship on more critical areas of the event (registration was free, remember). The ROM's cafeteria will be open 11am - 4:30pm, and there are great local breakfast and dinner restaurants, bars, coffee shops, and hot dog stands within 5 minutes walking distance.
How will teams form? Can I pitch another challenge?
Challenges will be briefly reviewed on stage Friday, and teams will be encouraged to organize around challenge-specific tables and start working that evening. If you want to work on a challenge that isn't part of the 24 that we have chosen to work on locally, you will be asked to quickly pitch (1 min) the challenge you'd like to work on, and the talents you're looking to recruit. If you're working on a challenge and want to pitch to get some extra excitement going for your challenge, you can also opt to pitch, but it's not necessary.
How will we be judged? Will there be any prizes?
The Space Apps Challenge is focused on collaboration, not competition. Judges will choose one "Best of" team from each of four categories: Visualization, Software, Hardware, and Citizen Science. Two of those teams will also be submitted to NASA for "Best of" consideration on the International level. We are planning on providing small prizes for winners, but no official major prizes other than an awesome title and probably some fun press.
What are the mentors doing? How can I schedule them?
Expert mentors in Design, Aerospace, and Development will be available on Saturday and Sunday to assist teams. All participants are encouraged to use mentors throughout the weekend, and to book them in 20 minute slots on this Google Doc, before and during the event.
